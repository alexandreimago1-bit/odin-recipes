Smoky Grilled Pork Chops

Why You'll Love This

  • This recipe creates a quick homemade seasoning rub to bring smoky, spicy flavor to these chops.
  • Home cooks like using smoked paprika or charcoal to achieve juicy results every time.
  • Allrecipes member UpbeatFish1828 confirms, "It comes together very quickly."

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons seasoned salt (such as Lawry's)
  • 2 tablespoons garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons onion powder
  • 2 tablespoons ground paprika
  • 4 teaspoons Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 teaspoons ground black pepper
  • 2 teaspoons liquid smoke flavoring
  • 8 bone-in pork chops ( ½- to ¾-inch thick )

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat an outdoor grill for indirect cooking over medium heat and lightly oil the grate.
  2. To make the wet spice rub: Mix seasoned salt, garlic powder, onion powder, paprika, Worcestershire sauce, pepper, and liquid smoke together in a small bowl until thoroughly combined.
  3. Massage spice rub on both sides of pork chops; let stand for 10 minutes.
  4. Cook chops on the preheated grill over indirect heat until no longer pink inside, about 12 minutes per side. An instant-read thermometer should read at least 145 degrees F (63 degrees C).
  5. Let stand for 10 minutes before serving.

Note
I like to serve these grilled pork chops with loaded baked potatoes and a green salad.
